DATA PROTECTION & PRIVACY POLICY

Marshal Board Committee (MBC) | Marshal Core of Nigeria

1. Introduction

The MARSHAL BOARD COMMITTEE (MBC) is a Federal Executive Body under MARSHAL CORE OF NIGERIA who are responsible to:
(a) Appoint persons to offices (other than the Office of the FOUNDER OF MARSHAL) in the Marshal core of Nigeria;
(b) Dismiss and exercise disciplinary control over persons holding any office referred to in sub-paragraph (a) of this paragraph.
The MBC is committed to safeguarding the privacy and security of personal information. This Data Protection and Privacy Policy outlines our obligation to respecting and fully protecting the personal data of individuals and complies with applicable data protection laws across all the jurisdictions in which we operate.

2. Definitions

Personal Information: Personal Information consists of any data that relates to an individual and/or information from which an individual can be identified, directly or indirectly. It includes but is not limited to names, contact details, date of birth, photographs, and biometric data, such as facial recognition data.

3. Data Collection

3.1 Types of Personal Information Collected

This Commission, in the discharge of its Constitutional Mandate, may collect, use, store, and transfer different kinds of Personal Information about applicants. This includes, but not limited to:

  • Names
  • Contact details
  • Date of birth
  • Photographs
  • Academic Records
  • Next of Kin details
  • Local Government of Origin
  • State of Origin
  • Past Employment information
  • Biometric data (including facial recognition data)

3.2 Consent and Opt-In/Opt-Out

When collecting personal data, including biometric, facial recognition, fingerprints, or other unique biological identifiers, the MARSHAL BOARD COMMITTEE shall obtain the explicit, informed, and unambiguous consent of the data subject before any processing occurs. By voluntarily providing such data, individuals consent to its use solely for the stated and legitimate purposes as outlined in this policy. Consent shall be freely given, specific, informed, and revocable at any time. Data subjects reserve the right to withdraw their consent or opt-out of data processing at any time, without any negative consequences. To exercise this right, individuals may contact the MBC Data Protection Officer (DPO).

Data Protection Officer Contact:

info@marshalcoreofnigeria.ng

3.3 How Personal Information is Collected

The MBC typically collects information through various means, including but not limited to:

  • Candidates' applications online
  • Information obtained from third parties, such as the National Identity Management Commission (NIMC), the MARSHAL CORE OF NIGERIA, past employers, regulatory bodies, and members of the public.

4. Purpose of Data Collection

The MBC uses the Personal Information, including biometric data, for the following purposes:

  • Screening of candidates to determine suitability for appointment
  • Shortlisting of candidates for Physical and Credential examination
  • Shortlisting of candidates for Aptitude Tests
  • Final Selection of successful candidates in recruitment exercises
  • Fulfilling promotion, disciplinary and human resource management functions

5. Sharing of Personal Information

The MBC may share Personal Information, including biometric data, with Third-party service providers, including but not limited to IT service providers and the MARSHAL CORE OF NIGERIA for processing, human resource management and mandate fulfillment interests. Consequently, the MBC will never sell or disclose any Personal Information to third parties.

6. Data Security

MBC implements appropriate physical and technical measures to safeguard the Personal Information. Access is restricted to authorized personnel subject to confidentiality. No system is entirely secure, hence the MBC cannot guarantee the security of Personal Information.

7. Data Retention

MBC retains Personal Information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, in accordance with applicable laws.

8. Data Subject Rights

  • Right to request access to and copies of their data (Subject Access)
  • Right to withdraw consent (if applicable)
  • Right to data portability (under certain circumstances)
  • Right to erasure in specific situations

9. International Data Transfers

The PSC may transfer Personal Information across borders when necessary. These transfers will be carried out in accordance with applicable data protection regulations.

10. Compliance with Specific Regulations

The MBC commits to compliance with all applicable data protection regulations in Nigeria, including but not limited to GDPR and the Nigeria Data Protection Act.

11. Changes to this Policy

The MBC may update this policy from time to time. Updates will be effective upon posting the revised policy on our Recruitment Portal.
